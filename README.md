# Fosty

[Fosty](https://fosty.us) is a foster coordination platform for animal shelters and rescues, founded by [Evan Luebbert](https://github.com/luebbertevan) and built in partnership with [Colorado Kitty Coalition](https://www.cokittycoalition.com/).

## Why it exists

Before moving to NYC, Evan fostered 34 kittens with CKC. Their team struggled with volunteer coordination and urgent care communication split across texts, email chains, Instagram DMs, and messy Google Sheets. In rescue, that friction can seriously affect outcomes: animals in critical condition need timely intervention, and scattered communication introduces serious risk.

When Evan proposed Fosty to the CKC, they were eager to collaborate—“We need you!”

## What Fosty is

Fosty reduces administrative chaos by providing **one place** for intake records, placement workflow, and staff–foster communication. It is built for **real operations**: diverse intake data, litters and singles, and the need for clear timelines.

Data is **scoped per organization**, so each rescue’s animals, people, and conversations stay separate—ready for more than one partner.

## Features

### Animal & intake records

- **Profiles** for each animal: status (e.g., needs foster, in foster, medical hold, adopted), photos, care and medical notes, and other fields rescues already track on paper or in spreadsheets.
- **Search and filters** so coordinators can sort records by status, species, tags, and more.
- **Bulk add** for coordinators when many animals arrive at once.
- **Timelines** of updates so everyone sees what changed and when, not just the latest text thread.

### Groups (e.g., litters)

- Animals can belong to a **group** (such as a litter) with shared context while keeping **individual** profiles for tracking.
- Coordinators manage groups alongside singles; fosters see assignments in a way that matches how they actually take animals home.

### Foster opportunities & assignments

- **Fosters Needed** is the board where open placements live; **requests** and **assignments** are how “I’m interested” becomes a confirmed household—without losing context in side channels.

### Foster directory

- **Foster directory**: profiles with contact details, assignments, requests, and more—so coordinators have operational visibility across volunteers.

### Roles & access

- **Coordinator** and **foster** experiences are tailored to the user: coordinators see roster-wide tools, messaging oversight, and placement queues; fosters see **their** assignments, opportunities to help, and direct lines to staff.

## Requests & assignments

- Fosters browse **Fosters Needed** and submit a **request** for a specific animal or group. Coordinators review **pending requests** in one workflow—approve, reject, or mark fulfilled—instead of piecing together DMs and screenshots.
- **Assignments** move animals (or an entire **group**, when a litter stays together) onto a foster and update **status** and **visibility** so the roster and the placement board stay aligned.

### Messaging

- **Group chats** between fosters and staff, with **full visibility for coordinators** so the whole team can stay in the loop without anyone living in a private text bubble.
- **Messaging is tied into the rest of the app**, not a separate inbox: **tags** connect threads to the relevant **animals** and **groups** and carry **workflow context** into chat—so coordination stays anchored to real records instead of getting lost in a generic group thread.
